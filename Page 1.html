Premise of Topic

The computer revolution made its impact back in the 1960s, and the decades after the revolution have significantly shaped society. However, how did the computer revolution change society as a whole? Some people have opinions that the computer revolution completely changed for the better, while others say that the computer revolution didn't make society better. Others say that the computer revolution's impact created a balanced society due to the weights of positives and negatives from the revolution, or people's opinions on the revolution changed depending on where they are in society. Some people focused on the past when the revolution happened and when it was great, while others focused on the future and its great technological growth. Where does society stand now after all the decades of computer changes? That is what I am going to answer here.


Also, a quick note from the information of sources used to present opinions, the computer revolution is centered around the dramatic development of computers and its impact towards society. What this means is that this revolution also affects the future of technology as a whole. Therefore, the mention of technology in society will be made, but only in the leading future and not at the start of the computer revolution.
