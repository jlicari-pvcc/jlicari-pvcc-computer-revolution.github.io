Conclusion

In conclusion, the computer revolution, originating back in the 1960s, has immensely shaped society over the many decades with debates surrounding its overall impact. While the computer revolution has created efficiency with workflow, enhanced communication, and supported education and research, people still bring concerns regarding computer system threats, job displacement, and the negative mental and physical effects on someone due to excessive screen time. Despite these challenges, the majority of the public recognizes the benefical impacts of the revolution, while a small minority of the public emphasizes its negative impacts. As technology continues to evolve to new complexities and opportunities, utilization of these advancements can overall be beneficial from the computer revolution. Since society is filled with individual perspectives, the true impact that the computer revolution made in society is through themselves.
