My Opinions

So, where do I stand in this premise surrounding the computer revolution? Personally, I can confidently say that the computer revolution and its continuous development throughout my lifetime has benefited society as a whole. Despite the numerous arguments that negatively downplay how computers in the future changed society for the worse, there are always solutions to fix the main issues in the first place using computers and common sense. Can't find a job because computers took over? Why not work in the computer industry? If it isn't your interest, then start a new business that isn't ridiculed by computers and it might create an economic boom for yourself. Computers became complex to learn? Sure, computers back then had limited functionality and systems that made then simple to learn and understand. Nowadays, learning multiple programming languages is an essential skill to get into the technological industry. Computers created a horrible lifestyle? Take some responsibiity and control to balance your computer usage times and your personal life. I honestly don't see any problems when computers revolutionized the past and future.


Does that mean that I'm correct and all of this resarch done means nothing? Definitely not. First, this was my personal opinion, meaning that you don't have to agree with what I'm saying. Second, gathering different viewpoints is a great way to picture out a debatable situation. In the case of this topic, sometimes the obvious answer isn't the true answer. The impacts the computer revolution made on society is dependent on a person's current position in society. People range from being young to old, or from being economically poor to rich. This also includes people's experiences with computers, whether they've been previously a contributor to the revolution or not, there is bias.
